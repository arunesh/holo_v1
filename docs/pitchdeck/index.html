The shift

From watching to inhabiting

A video stops being a strip of frames. It becomes a live scene, a story told over it, and a vocabulary of things that can happen.

▶ WATCH

The authored narration plays its beats over the scene, like any film — camera moves, highlights, and voiceover, in order.

⦿ ASK

Your voice goes to a model that drives the same scene with the same commands — go anywhere, ask anything, mid-story.

Technical work · open by design

Three problems, built in the open

FORMAT

An open encoding for interactive video

What mp4 did for pixels, done for worlds: one portable file carrying the scene, the narration, and the semantics of what can happen. Our v1 ships today as pods — a parameterized scene, narration beats, and a library of named actions.

PLAYER

A player that is a model

Tomorrow's player doesn't decode frames — it understands them. A vision-language model holds the scene, renders whatever you ask to see, and answers as the video, including moments no author ever filmed.

INTERACTION

A grammar for talking to video

Pause and ask. Zoom past the frame. Change a parameter and watch the story recompute. Branch the plot. The remote control becomes a conversation.

Markets

Where inhabited video wins first

EDUCATION

Every explainer becomes a lab

The lecture you watch is the system you interrogate — one scene answers both. A decade of YouTube back-catalog is the content library; our pipeline already turns a URL into an interactive pod.

CREATIVE EXPRESSION

Creators ship worlds, not renders

An authored scene holds every camera path a viewer will ever want. An open format makes remixing a right, not a hack.

CROWD-SOURCED STORYTELLING

Stories that fork

Viewers don't just watch the plot — they branch it. Communities co-author persistent worlds; canon becomes a graph, not a strip.
